MaterialIQ

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

MaterialIQ is a browser extension that reads the product page you are currently viewing and shows you a value score based on material quality, construction, and comparable pricing. We built it to respect your privacy. This policy explains exactly what the extension does and does not do with data.

The short version: MaterialIQ does its scoring on your own device. By default it sends nothing to any server, shows no ads, and does not track you, sell your data, or build a profile of you.

What the extension reads

When you open a supported product page, MaterialIQ reads publicly visible details from that page in order to score it, such as:

  • Product title and description
  • Listed price
  • Material composition (for example, "100% cotton")
  • Publicly shown product reviews on the page

This reading happens locally in your browser. The extension does not read passwords, payment information, form inputs, or any content unrelated to scoring the product.

What is stored

Your settings (such as whether optional AI review summaries are enabled) are saved locally using the browser's storage API. This data stays in your browser and is not transmitted to us.

Optional AI review summaries

MaterialIQ includes an off-by-default feature that can summarize a product's reviews using AI. If — and only if — you turn this on in the extension's settings, the extension sends the product URL and publicly visible review text to the MaterialIQ backend, which uses a third-party AI provider (Anthropic) to generate a short summary. This request contains no personal identifiers, no account information, and no browsing history. If you leave this feature off, no data ever leaves your device.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell or rent your data to anyone.
  • We do not use your data for advertising.
  • We do not track your browsing across sites.
  • We do not collect names, emails, or account credentials.

Permissions, briefly

  • storage — save your settings locally.
  • activeTab / scripting — read the product page you are viewing so it can be scored.
  • sidePanel — show the score in Chrome's side panel.
  • Host access — run on shopping pages so the score can appear where you shop.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the extension handles data, we will update this page and the "last updated" date above.
